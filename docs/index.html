Justification

“Anthropometric indicators most commonly used to assess the nutritional status of children are weight-for-age, height-for-age, and weight-for-height. These indices reflect different aspects of growth and nutritional status. Weight-for-age is a composite index influenced by both height and body mass; height-for-age reflects linear growth and is an indicator of long-term nutritional status; weight-for-height reflects body mass in relation to height and is particularly useful for identifying wasting.”1

  1. World Health Organization. Physical status: the use and interpretation of anthropometry. Report of a WHO Expert Committee. Geneva: World Health Organization; 1995. (WHO Technical Report Series No. 854). https://www.who.int/nutrition/publications/infantfeeding/WHO_NHD_01.9/en/

Indicators

Each indicator measures a different aspect of growth and nutritional status.

  • Weight-for-age (W/A) → detects underweight
  • Height-for-age (H/A) → detects growth retardation (chronic malnutrition)
  • Weight-for-height (W/H) → detects acute malnutrition

Recommendations and Conclusions

  1. Outliers should be filtered by age and gender
  2. COUNTRY A has the lowest weight and height
  3. BMI for females increases earlier
  4. BMI for males increases later
  5. Outliers are present and influential at the extremes

Summary

In general, children (aged 1 to 5 years) show the expected increase in height with age, but the levels vary depending on the context: country A consistently shows lower weight and height. Differences between the sexes are evident in the timing of changes in BMI—earlier among girls and later among boys—but are smaller than the differences observed between countries. The map confirms the geographical heterogeneity in average BMI, and the presence of outliers and a large sample size of 5-year-old children help explain some characteristics of the distribution. Taken together, these findings highlight significant variations between countries and across space in early childhood growth within the dataset in question.
